Meta-Learning System

Open before each study session to align your study habits with cognitive science principles.

M1

Active Recall Check

After completing each topic, close all tabs and notes. Explain the concept aloud or write it down from memory. Any gap in memory is a signal to immediately restudy.

M2

Feynman Technique

Explain the topic in plain, simple words as if teaching a beginner. If you must resort to jargon or fail to find a simple analogy, you haven't fully learned it.

M3

Anki / Flashcards

Use flashcards ONLY for things you repeatedly forget (e.g. math formulas, exact terms, specific dimensions). Do not front-load general theory.

M4

Interleaving

For every study session, mix old concepts with new ones (e.g., 1 old topic review + 1 new topic block) to prevent passive recognition and strengthen long-term recall.

M5

Build & Explain

After building any ML project or step, force yourself to explain why each part works, what each line of code accomplishes, and what would happen if you changed parameters.

M6

Spaced Repetition

Revisit each finished topic at exactly ~1 week, ~1 month, and ~3 months intervals. Set up reminders or track dates to keep the memory pathways active.

M7

3-Layer Attack

For any hard concept: obtain Intuition (3Blue1Brown/StatQuest), read the Primary Teaching source, then do the Implementation (write pure NumPy code/derivations). Do these in pairs, not sequentially.

M8

Build-then-Explain Loop

Immediately follow Abhishek's "paper-to-code loop": derive on paper first, code from scratch, and then write/speak a clear explanation of how the math translates to lines of code.

M9

Teach to Retain

If a concept continues to slip out of memory, write an article, make a short video, or teach the concept aloud to someone else. The act of packaging the idea for another brain seals it in yours.

★

Keystone Rule: Paper First

Derive before building. Do not touch code until you have mapped the equations, dimensions, and derivatives on paper. Photograph your derivations to keep a visual proof-log.
